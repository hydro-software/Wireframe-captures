Suggested sheet layout — 1 × poplar ply 242 × 120 cm

All cabinet panels nested on a single 242 × 120 cm sheet (15 mm poplar). The soffit and monitor shelf stack in the column above the roof — that previously empty 43 cm gap above the 52.2 cm roof now carries two more panels. The mouse shelf takes the top band over the side panels. The logo panel (acrylic) is sourced separately.

Side panel — home cuts

From a 50 × 95 cm rectangle, mark points A–F, then jigsaw the orange waste triangles (front-tilt + soffit as one L-shaped cut, plus the roof slope), and the mouse-shelf slot — a closed rectangle sitting 3 cm back from the tilted front so the front-edge wood stays continuous between conformats. Drill a starter hole inside the slot, then jigsaw the perimeter. Faint dashed rectangles show the alternative slot heights — pick one.

Tip for the wood shop: ask them to cut through the grain of the top layer so the panel-saw blade scoring keeps the visible edges chip-free. Good shops do this by default — mention it if you're not sure.

Knock-Down Assembly — Folding Roof + French-Cleat Front

Tool-free 3-minute assembly, survives many transport cycles. The hinges are split across BOTH side panels so each sub-assembly stays within its own polygon footprint: Side R + Roof are hinged together (Roof folds flat against Side R via a long piano hinge along D→E). Side L + two horizontal cross-rails form the second sub-assembly — the rails (~54 cm long, 4 × 3 cm cross-section) hinge to Side L's interior face at corners A and B and fold up/down so they sit entirely within Side L's polygon. When deployed, the rails bridge across to Side R and bolt with wing nuts. The Front panel hangs from a French cleat on the upper rail — no fasteners, no tools, slides on under its own weight.

Skeleton — 45° perspective (deployed)

Just the four structural pieces: two side panels, the roof (hinged to Side R along D→E), and the two cross-rails (hinged to Side L at corners A and B, bolted to Side R at the free ends). No shelves, no front panel, no logo — this is what holds the cabinet square. Everything else hangs off this frame.

Sub-assembly 1 · Side R + Roof — folded

Roof folds down along the D→E piano hinge. With the LOGO_Z = 10 cm recess pulling corner D inward, the roof's folded projection (shown dashed) sits almost entirely within Side R's polygon — sub-assembly bounding box ≈ 50.6 × 95 cm, plus ~3 cm of stack thickness from the two stacked panels and the piano hinge.

Sub-assembly 2 · Side L + 2 cross-rails — folded

Beam 1 hinges at corner A (front-bottom) and folds up along Side L's front edge. Beam 2 hinges at corner B (top of the tilted-front cut) and folds down. Both rails sit entirely within Side L's polygon — no overhang, sub-assembly bounding box ≈ 50 × 95 cm.

French-cleat detail · Beam 2 ↔ Front D

Cross-section in the depth-height plane at the top of the front panel. Beam 2 has the wall cleat ripped directly into its front edge as a 45° bevel — one piece of stock (7 × 4 cm hardwood, 54 cm long) with one rip cut on a table saw replaces the previous beam + glued-cleat-strip approach. No glue joint at this cleat, stronger, faster to make. Cleat B (the wedge glued to Front D's back) stays a separate strip — that joint is permanent and never comes apart. Beam 2 is mounted with the same 20° tilt as Front D so the bevel face and cleat B sit on parallel tilted surfaces and mate at the same diagonal angle. Drop Front D in: cabinet cleat slides down onto the integrated bevel, gravity locks them. The vertical reference + 20° angle arc next to the pivot make the tilt explicit.

Assembly sequence

  1. Open both sub-assemblies. Sub-assembly 1: unfold Roof from Side R around the D→E piano hinge. Sub-assembly 2: unfold Beam 1 (rotates down from Side L's front-bottom corner A) and Beam 2 (rotates up from Side L's tilt-top corner B), both to horizontal. ~15 sec.
  2. Stand the two side panels facing each other. The free ends of Beam 1 and Beam 2 (on Side L) reach across to Side R's interior face at the matching corners. Bolt each beam end to Side R with a corner bracket + 2 wing nuts. ~1 min.
  3. Drop Soffit (G) into the dadoes between the two side panels at Y = 77 cm. Tighten 4 wing nuts at the bracket corners. ~30 sec.
  4. Drop Monitor shelf (H) into its dadoes at Y = 38 cm. 4 more wing nuts. ~30 sec.
  5. Slide Mouse shelf (M) through the front-edge slots in both side panels. Friction-fit — no fasteners. ~10 sec.
  6. Lower Front panel (D) down onto the cleat on Beam 2. Slides into place, locks by gravity. ~5 sec.
  7. Clip Logo (F, acrylic + foamboard + LEDs) onto the back of the soffit overhang or hang from a third small cleat. ~10 sec.

Total: ~3 minutes for one person, no tools beyond hand-tightening wing nuts. Disassembly is the same in reverse — pull the front panel off, undo 8–12 wing nuts, fold the sub-assembly. Repeats indefinitely without thread wear because the wing-nut threads engage barrel nuts (cross-dowels) embedded in the side panels, not raw plywood.

Crate as Booth Table

The crate doubles as the booth table the cabinet sits on. Make the crate 40 cm tall (deeper than the panel stack needs — the extra space stores the PC, monitor, cables and accessories). Split the top lid into two halves; each half hinges on one of the crate's short ends and folds open to form a trestle-style leg. A turning-stick latch on each short side locks the leg upright. Flip the crate over (legs down) and you have a 90–95 cm tall booth table — crate body 40 cm + leg ~54 cm.

Closed · top view (transport)

Lid is two equal halves split across the long axis. Hinges live on the short sides — flush-mount butt hinges or piano hinges, mounted to the OUTSIDE so the lid can swing 180° outward without binding on the crate wall.

Deployed · table mode (side view)

Lift each lid 90° outward, rotate the turning stick to lock it perpendicular, then flip the whole crate so the original bottom is now the table surface. Each leg is 54 cm tall × 56 cm wide × 1.5 cm thick — the wide footprint at each short end gives a stable trestle base. The cabinet bolts to the top surface via the same wing-nut + cross-dowel scheme as the rest of the build.

Turning-stick latch · two states

Plan view at the leg-to-crate joint. A short wooden pivot mounts through the crate's short-side wall, with a handle on the inside and a tongue on the outside. In transport, the tongue lies flat against the wall (parallel) so the lid can close. To deploy: rotate the handle 90°; the tongue swings perpendicular and engages a small notch cut into the leg's inside face, locking the leg in its upright (table-deployed) position. Two latches per leg — one near the front edge of the crate, one near the back — keep the leg from rocking.

Order-of-operations on arrival: (1) unlatch the lids · (2) swing each lid 90° upward to vertical · (3) rotate both turning sticks to lock the legs · (4) tip the crate end-over-end so the legs land on the floor · (5) open the (now-bottom) crate door, take out the cabinet sub-assemblies, monitor, PC · (6) build the cabinet on top of the table. Total table-deployment: under a minute.
