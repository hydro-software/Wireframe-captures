Suggested sheet layout — 1 × poplar ply 242 × 120 cm

All cabinet panels nested on a single 242 × 120 cm sheet (15 mm poplar). The soffit and monitor shelf stack in the column above the roof — that previously empty 43 cm gap above the 52.2 cm roof now carries two more panels. The mouse shelf takes the top band over the side panels. The logo panel (acrylic) is sourced separately.

Side panel — home cuts

From a 50 × 95 cm rectangle, mark points A–F, then make three jigsaw cuts: the orange waste triangles (front-tilt + soffit as one L-shaped cut, plus the roof slope), and the mouse-shelf slot opening at the tilted front edge. Faint dashed rectangles show the alternative slot heights — pick one.

Tip for the wood shop: ask them to cut through the grain of the top layer so the panel-saw blade scoring keeps the visible edges chip-free. Good shops do this by default — mention it if you're not sure.

Knock-Down Assembly — Conformat Bolts

The cabinet is held together with conformat bolts (also called cross-dowel + machine-screw, or barrel-nut bolts — the same fastener every IKEA cabinet uses). One Allen key, no glue, hundreds of assembly cycles. Each joint shows up as a single hole drilled through the side panel face into the mating panel's edge.

Side panel — conformat positions (×2, mirror)

Drill these from the inside face of each side panel. Mirror the second side. Counter-bore the bolt-side face slightly so the bolt heads sit flush.

Hardware shopping list

JointPer sideTotal

Buy 24 conformat sets (20 needed + 4 spares).
M6 × 50 mm machine bolt + barrel nut, sold in packs of 4–10 at any hardware shop or online (search "vis conformat" / "cross-dowel bolt"). One 6 mm Allen key handles all of them.

Drilling guide: bolt hole 7 mm ⌀ through the side panel face. Cross-dowel hole 10 mm ⌀ × ~25 mm deep, drilled perpendicular into the mating panel's edge, so the bolt threads engage the cross-dowel sitting inside.

Shelf-on-side joints (G, H) also get a shallow dado: a 1.5 cm × 0.5 cm groove cut across the inside face of the side panel where each shelf lands. The shelf slides into the groove for shear strength (carries the weight without depending on the bolts), and the conformats clamp it from pulling out. No glue needed — the dado is a dry friction fit. Standard router bit, one pass per groove.
