Side panel — home cuts

From a 50 × 95 cm rectangle, mark points A–F, then jigsaw the orange waste areas: cut 1 is one continuous L-shaped cut A→B→C→D combining the front-tilt diagonal (A→B), the soffit horizontal (B→C), and the 10 cm logo-recess vertical (C→D) so the top-front corner sits 10 cm back from the front face for screen sight-line. Then cut 2 (roof slope D→E). No mouse-shelf slot any more — the side mouse ledges (L) bracket onto the outer face of this panel instead; the ledge mount footprint is marked at the front edge.

Tip for the wood shop: ask them to cut through the grain of the top layer so the panel-saw blade scoring keeps the visible edges chip-free. Good shops do this by default — mention it if you're not sure.

Knock-Down Assembly — Folding Roof + Screwed Front

Quick assembly, survives many transport cycles. The cabinet ships as one folding sub-assembly — Side R + Roof, hinged together so the Roof folds flat against Side R via a long piano hinge along D→E — plus loose panels. Side L travels as a plain loose panel. On site the two side panels are stood up facing each other and the Front panel is screwed straight onto their front edges: that single panel ties the frame square — the earlier cross-beams and French cleat turned out to be unnecessary once the front panel does the racking work. The Roof's free edge then screws to Side L to lock the top.

Skeleton — 45° perspective (deployed)

The three structural pieces: two side panels and the roof (hinged to Side R along D→E). No cross-beams — the front panel (not shown here; see the front + perspective views) screws across the two side-panel front edges and squares the frame. Shelves, ledges and logo hang off this frame.

Assembly sequence

  1. Unfold the Roof from Side R around the D→E piano hinge (sub-assembly 1). ~10 sec.
  2. Stand Side R (with Roof) and Side L facing each other. ~10 sec.
  3. Screw the Front panel (D) onto the front edges of both side panels — roughly 8 screws per side. This is the main structural tie: it squares the frame. ~2 min.
  4. Screw the Roof's free edge down onto Side L's top edge to lock the top. ~30 sec.
  5. Drop Soffit (G) into the dadoes between the two side panels at Y = 77 cm and screw the ends. ~45 sec.
  6. Drop Monitor shelf (H) into its dadoes at Y = 38 cm and screw the ends. ~45 sec.
  7. Bolt the side mouse ledges (L) onto the outer face of each side panel — one per side, two bolts each. ~30 sec.
  8. Clip Logo (F, acrylic + foamboard + LEDs) onto the back of the soffit overhang. ~10 sec.

Total: ~5 minutes for one person with a screwdriver / drill. Disassembly is the same in reverse — back out the screws, lift off the panels, fold the Roof onto Side R. The front-edge screw holes take a few cycles of wear; if you expect heavy reuse, drop threaded inserts into the front-edge screw line.

Crate as Folding Stand

The transport crate folds inside-out to become the stand the arcade sits on — no separate table needed. The crate (95 × 56 × 18 cm, holding the flat art panels in transit) is built from a hinged panel whose hinge layout folds two ways: closed into the 56 × 18 cm crate tube, or open into a ⊓ stood on end. Stood up, the crate's 95 cm length becomes the ~94 cm stand height; a drop-in top panel gives the 58 × 50 cm surface the arcade rests on.

The hinged panel — one layout, two folds

A single 158 cm panel with six hinge lines at 50 / 56 / 74 / 108 / 130 / 148 cm. The stand fold (solid hinges, 50 & 108) groups it 50 | 58 | 50 — the ⊓ footprint. The crate fold (dashed hinges, 56 / 74 / 130) groups it 56 | 18 | 56 | 18, wrapping the crate tube, with the last 10 cm as the closing flap. The two hinge sets are disjoint, so each hinge only ever folds in one mode and stays locked flat in the other.

Crate mode · transport

Folded 56 | 18 | 56 | 18, the panel wraps a 56 × 18 cm rectangular tube, 95 cm long — it holds the flat art panels. The 10 cm flap folds to the inside (~8.5 cm laps flat) and screws to a long side panel. Two long side panels (22 × 99 cm — 18 + 4 by 95 + 4, sized 2 cm oversize on each edge) screw onto the long 56-cm faces, overlapping the borders. The two stand-fold hinges sit inside the long faces, locked flat.

Stand mode · deployed

Refolded 50 | 58 | 50, the panel opens into a ⊓ — a 58 cm back wall + two 50 cm-deep side walls, open at the front. Stood on end it is ~94 cm tall (the crate's 95 cm length). Two loose 58 × 50 cm panels drop in — the top panel the arcade sits on, and a matching bottom panel that closes the floor of the ⊓.

Suggested sheet layout — stand panels

The five stand panels nested on one 242 × 120 cm sheet: P the 158 × 95 cm hinged panel (dotted lines mark the six hinge cuts at 50 / 56 / 74 / 108 / 130 / 148 cm), T and B the 58 × 50 cm drop-in top and bottom panels, and S1 / S2 the two 22 × 99 cm long side panels (18 + 4 by 95 + 4 — 2 cm oversize on each edge so they overlap the tube's borders when screwed on). S2 is laid vertically to fit alongside B in the right column.

Order-of-operations on arrival: (1) unlatch the 10 cm flap and unwrap the crate tube · (2) refold the panel along the stand hinges (50 & 108) into the ⊓ · (3) stand it on end · (4) drop the top panel onto the ⊓ · (5) set the arcade on top — the art panels that travelled inside the crate go straight onto the cabinet. Still open: the exact hardware that locks the ⊓ rigid and fixes the top panel — flat-locks at each hinge plus the load-path for the arcade's weight still need detailing before this is a build spec.
